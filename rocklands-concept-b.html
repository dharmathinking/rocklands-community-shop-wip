Ways to get involved

Volunteer roles

Serving customers, operating the till, answering queries, keeping shelves stocked.

Making drinks, serving food and cakes, clearing tables, making our café welcoming.

Receiving deliveries, checking stock, rotating products, managing supplier relationships.
📣 Communications & admin
Newsletter, social media, website updates, governance documents and member communications.
🎉 Events
Helping organise community events — from chess afternoons to seasonal celebrations.
📋 Governance support
Supporting the Management Committee with meetings, minute-taking and policy reviews.

Your rights as a volunteer

  • To be treated with dignity and respect at all times
  • To receive all training necessary to carry out your role safely
  • To be covered by the Society's Public Liability insurance while volunteering
  • To have a clearly defined role with support from the staff team
  • To raise concerns through our formal channels
  • To be supported by our Equality & Diversity and Safeguarding policies

Intellectual property: Volunteers agree that any IP created whilst volunteering is assigned to Rocklands Community Shop Ltd (the Society), which retains ownership.

Who we are

About The Rocklands

A Community Benefit Society trading for the benefit of the local community. All profits remain within the Society.

Our mission

Mission Statement

"We pledge to operate our business in an ethical, responsible, and sustainable way for the benefit of the local community and community projects as appropriate."

We source within a 30-mile radius or across Norfolk wherever possible. We only obtain meat from Red Tractor assured suppliers.

Signed by all members of the Management Committee. Dated: 1 August 2013.

Our values

H·R·Q·A·E

H
Honesty
Building strong relationships based on trust — speaking honestly with our suppliers, community and each other.
R
Responsibility
Actively encouraging everyone to improve our service, always in the best interests of the community.
Q
Quality
Providing and continually improving a quality service to all our stakeholders.
A
Awareness
Acknowledging the social importance of the shop — especially for the elderly and rurally isolated hamlets.
E
Ethics
Believing strongly in animal welfare, fair trade and social involvement — without prejudice.
Membership

Own your community shop

The Rocklands is a Community Benefit Society. Membership is how the Society is owned by the community. Members attend and vote at Members' Meetings. The Management Committee is elected by and accountable to members.

Complaints

Complaints Procedure

We view complaints as an opportunity to learn and improve. Formal complaints must be submitted in writing to the Management Committee or Chair.

Stage 1 — Initial review

Acknowledged within 5 working days. Written response within 28 days.

Stage 2 — Further review

If still dissatisfied, write to the Chair. A panel will respond within 28 days.

Contact for formal complaints

Post: The Management Committee, Rocklands Community Shop Ltd, The Street, Rocklands, Norfolk, NR17 1HP. Or email via our , marking it 'Formal Complaint'.
