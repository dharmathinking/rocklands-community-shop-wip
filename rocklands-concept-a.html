Community served

Volunteers are the lifeblood of Rocklands Market. Every shift contributed makes a real difference to a community that depends on this shop.

Ways to get involved

🛒 Shop floor & till
Serving customers, operating the till, answering queries, keeping shelves stocked and tidy.
☕ Café team
Making drinks, serving food and cakes, clearing tables, and making our café a welcoming place.
📦 Stock & deliveries
Receiving deliveries, checking stock, rotating products and managing supplier relationships.
📣 Communications & admin
Helping with the newsletter, social media, website updates and member communications.
🎉 Events
Helping organise community events — from chess afternoons to seasonal celebrations.
📋 Governance support
Supporting the Management Committee with meetings, minute-taking and policy reviews.

Your rights as a volunteer

  • To be treated with dignity and respect at all times
  • To receive all training necessary to carry out your role safely
  • To be covered by the Society's Public Liability insurance while volunteering
  • To have a clearly defined role with support from the staff team
  • To raise concerns through our formal channels
  • To be supported by our Equality & Diversity and Safeguarding policies

Intellectual property: Volunteers agree that any IP created whilst volunteering is assigned to Rocklands Community Shop Ltd (the Society), which retains ownership.

Who we are

About Rocklands Market

A Community Benefit Society trading for the benefit of the local community. All profits remain within the Society.

Mission Statement

"We pledge to operate our business in an ethical, responsible, and sustainable way for the benefit of the local community and community projects as appropriate."

We will endeavour to source produce from within a 30-mile radius or across Norfolk whenever possible. We will only obtain meat from suppliers assured under the Red Tractor scheme.

Signed by all members of the Management Committee. Dated: 1 August 2013.

Our Values

H
Honesty
Building strong relationships based on trust — speaking honestly with our suppliers, community and each other.
R
Responsibility
Actively encouraging everyone to explore new ways of improving our service, always in the best interests of the community.
Q
Quality
Providing and continually improving a quality service to all our stakeholders.
A
Awareness
Acknowledging the social importance of the shop, especially for the elderly and rurally isolated hamlets.
E
Ethics
Believing strongly in animal welfare, fair trade and social involvement — encouraging input from any person without prejudice.

Membership

Rocklands Market is a Community Benefit Society. Membership is the means by which the Society is owned by the community. Members exercise their voice by attending and voting at Members' Meetings.

Privacy Policy

Rocklands Community Shop Ltd takes your privacy seriously.

  • We collect personal data only where necessary.
  • We will not share your information with third parties for marketing purposes.
  • Your data is stored securely in accordance with the UK GDPR.
  • You may request a copy of your data, corrections, or deletion at any time.
  • Our website uses cookies — you may accept or decline on your first visit.
  • Cardholder data is destroyed immediately after transactions are processed.

Complaints Procedure

How to make a complaint

We view complaints as an opportunity to learn and improve. Formal complaints must be submitted in writing (post or email) to the Management Committee or Chair.

Stage 1 — Initial review

Acknowledged within 5 working days. Written response within 28 days.

Stage 2 — Further review

If still dissatisfied, write to the Chair. A panel will respond within 28 days.

Contact for formal complaints

Post: The Management Committee, Rocklands Community Shop Ltd, The Street, Rocklands, Norfolk, NR17 1HP.
